nameless

"No names," she hisses, "yours or anyone else's."

Reaching over, she gives a rough swipe of the silver band around his wrist.
Without a chance to protest, his account balance appears, the numbers travelling well into six figures.

Ezri makes note of the serial number at the edge. 149907.
She flicks the holographic screen aside and turns on her heel, when he grasps her by the forearms, spinning her around to face him once more.

"Eika? Eika, you need to leave – go home. They're coming."

She rips his grip from her skin; one hand seizing the collar of his shirt, the palm of the other striking his cheek, leaving a red-hot mark in its wake.

"Now, listen here," she says, drawing him closer by her hold on the fabric. "I don't know how much they offered you to come in and cause trouble, but you’ll pay for it."

She gives a grin more startling than her shove, which sends him staggering backwards, as she nods to a guard by the door. "Consider it a warning."

Ezri strides back to her seat, unflinching as he calls the name again, cut off mid-scream by the guard's hand over his mouth as he's hauled out the exit.

~

Nex stands as she re-joins them, falling into place at her side. His shoulder brushes hers, the leather of his jacket coarse. He holds out a cigarette.
Ezri takes it between her teeth with a sigh, drawing a deep breath as he lights the tip.

"Get Jaz. We've got some hunting to do."
