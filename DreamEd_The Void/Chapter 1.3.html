nameless

Her chair faces the floor-to-ceiling windows, looking out onto the neon chaos of the city. The amaranth liquid swirls in its holder as she retrieves her umpteenth cigarette of the night.
What's bound to be a long one, she thinks, fumbling with the lighter.

Ezri leans back in her seat, replacing the burning lights with the dark of her eyelids.

She muses on the stranger's words, against her better judgement. Go home.

What was that before the four walls of her penthouse over the club?

She tries to conjure an image, a memory or sight apart from the one on the other side of the glass, a taste before the perpetual nicotine between her teeth, but the palms of her mind turn up empty.
A stream of smoke escapes her as she shakes the thought away, followed by a muttered curse — her focus turning towards a far-more confounding matter.

Who the hell is Eika?
