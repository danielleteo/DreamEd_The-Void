chapter 2

writing on the wall

The feeds flicker across the screen as they have for the past hour, the events of the night repeating themselves at twice the speed.

Patrons come and go. A faceless mass at the centre of the dancefloor. Swaying, as if caught in a trance between the heady air and embalming sound.
Nex squints at the displays, a strained attempt at sifting through the tangles of the crowd – affixed to one another by a sheen of light perspiration and near-instinctive bond – in hopes of identifying their unwelcome visitor.

He swaps the footage for that captured by the cameras at the door, revealing customers as they file in, IDs and bodies scanned before they are granted access. The entrance to the highest level of the club, the Throne, maintains a sparser stream of guests than its counterparts. Nex leans in, resuming the records at a normal pace, as he watches for his target.

There.
